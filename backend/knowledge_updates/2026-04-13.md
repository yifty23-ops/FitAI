- **New text:** `"10-20 sets per muscle group per week (dose-response continues with diminishing returns beyond ~20 sets), per Pelland et al. (2024) meta-regression of 67 studies — Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research"`

The 2024 paper is the most comprehensive meta-analysis on this topic to date and directly supersedes Schoenfeld et al. (2017) as the go-to reference.
- **Citation:** Pelland, J.C., et al. (2024). The Resistance Training Dose Response: Meta-Regressions Exploring the Effects of Weekly Volume and Frequency on Muscle Hypertrophy and Strength Gains. *Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research*. PubMed ID: 41343037. DOI: 10.1519/JSC.0000000000004982
- **Confidence:** HIGH (67 studies, 2,058 subjects; directly supersedes previous reference; published in JSCR)

### File: `backend/tiers.py`

**SPORT_DEMANDS["tennis"] — GIRD added**

- **What changed:** Added mention of glenohumeral internal rotation deficit (GIRD) management to the tennis sport demands description.
- **Old text:** `"rotational power, shoulder durability, lateral movement capacity, anti-rotation core strength, and tournament-schedule periodization. You understand the unique demands of a sport with no off-season and frequent travel."`
- **New text:** `"rotational power, shoulder durability (including dominant-side glenohumeral internal rotation deficit management, GIRD), lateral movement capacity, anti-rotation core strength, and tournament-schedule periodization. You understand the unique demands of a sport with no off-season and frequent travel."`
- **Why:** A 2024 systematic review analyzed shoulder range of motion in 18,534 competitive tennis players across 25 studies and confirmed that the dominant shoulder universally exhibits significantly reduced internal rotation compared to the non-dominant side. GIRD is an injury risk factor for rotator cuff pathology and SLAP lesions if not proactively managed through posterior shoulder stretching and rotator cuff strengthening. An elite tennis S&C coach who does not explicitly understand GIRD would produce incomplete shoulder health programming. The current entry referenced "shoulder durability" but omitted this specific, well-established structural adaptation.
- **Citation:** Genevois, C., et al. (2024). Shoulder range of motion in competitive tennis players: systematic review and meta-analysis. *Journal of Science and Medicine in Sport*. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsams.2024.01.009 (25 studies, n=18,534)
- **Confidence:** HIGH (large meta-analysis with 18,534 participants; GIRD is a foundational concept in overhead athlete shoulder health)

**SPORT_DEMANDS["mma"] — Weight management specificity added**

- **What changed:** Updated the MMA weight-cut reference from the generic "weight-cut preparation" to specify the evidence-based approach of gradual longitudinal weight descent, explicitly contrasting with acute dehydration-based cuts.
- **Old text:** `"strength-to-weight optimization, grip and neck strength, power endurance, fight-camp periodization, and weight-cut preparation. You understand how to peak strength and conditioning for a specific fight date while managing sparring load."`
- **New text:** `"strength-to-weight optimization, grip and neck strength, power endurance, fight-camp periodization, and evidence-based weight management (prioritizing gradual longitudinal weight descent over acute dehydration-based cuts to preserve strength and power into competition). You understand how to peak strength and conditioning for a specific fight date while managing sparring load."`
- **Why:** The International Society of Sports Nutrition (ISSN) published an official position stand in 2025 on nutrition and weight cut strategies for combat sports. Key findings: (1) >90% of MMA athletes currently use rapid dehydration-based cuts; (2) rapid weight loss is associated with reduced strength, power, and cognitive performance at competition; (3) gradual longitudinal weight descent (targeting 0.5–1% body mass loss per week across the fight camp) preserves lean mass, strength, and power significantly better than acute dehydration. An elite MMA S&C coach who only understands "weight-cut preparation" generically may not counsel athletes away from performance-damaging acute cuts. This update makes the coach's knowledge base specific and evidence-driven.
- **Citation:** Antonio, J., et al. (2025). International Society of Sports Nutrition position stand: nutrition and weight cut strategies for mixed martial arts and other combat sports. *Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition*. PubMed ID: 40059405. DOI: 10.1080/15502783.2025.2467909
- **Confidence:** HIGH (official ISSN position stand, peer-reviewed, directly addresses MMA weight management with performance implications)
